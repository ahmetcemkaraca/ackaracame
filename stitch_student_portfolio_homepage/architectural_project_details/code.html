A Sustainable Urban Housing Complex

A forward-thinking approach to community living in a dense urban environment.

Project Overview

This project explores the potential for high-density, sustainable housing within a constrained urban plot. The design prioritizes community interaction, natural light, and green spaces, creating a vertical neighborhood that enhances the quality of life for its residents. By integrating passive design strategies and renewable energy systems, the complex aims to achieve a near-net-zero energy footprint, setting a new benchmark for eco-friendly urban development. The architectural language is modern yet contextual, using locally sourced materials to blend with the surrounding cityscape while establishing its own unique identity.

Concept & Objectives

The core concept was to create a "living building" that breathes with the city. The design is organized around a central, light-filled atrium that acts as the social heart of the complex, encouraging spontaneous interactions between residents.

  • Maximize natural light and ventilation in all residential units.
  • Integrate shared amenities and green rooftop terraces to foster a strong sense of community.
  • Achieve high levels of energy efficiency through passive solar design and on-site generation.
  • Utilize a modular construction system to minimize waste and construction time.

Challenges & Solutions

The primary challenge was the narrow site, which was overshadowed by adjacent high-rise buildings. To overcome this, the building's form was sculpted through extensive solar analysis to optimize daylight access. The facade features a system of angled fins that bounce light deep into the interior spaces while also providing solar shading during peak hours. A rainwater harvesting system and greywater recycling were implemented to address water scarcity, significantly reducing the building's reliance on the municipal grid.
